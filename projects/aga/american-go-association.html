Responsive Redesign
The American Go Association
Redesigning AGA's website into a modern and responsive site.

Introduction

Go is an ancient board game, and one of my favorite games of all time. It's a game of strategy and intellect with endless complexity and depth. I love playing the game, and each year I participate in various events hosted by the American Go Association (AGA), an organization that promotes the public's participation in go.

However, the AGA's current official website is outdated and unresponsive across different device sizes. I decided to try to redesign it into a modern and responsive website that can help to promote go across the world.

Analyzing Design

To better understand how to improve the page, I analyzed the website based on 3 metrics: usability, learnability, and memorability.

Accessibility

Using WebAIM WAVE to analyze the original website, I found that various images are missing alternative text, forms are missing labels, and a few skip links are broken. There are also large blocks of justified text, which reduces readability. Overall, the website can be made much more accessible.

Using VoiceOver to test screenreader accessibility on the original website, I found that many words were repeated over and over by the screenreader, due to the repetitiveness of some menu items. Also, since sections on the page aren't very distinct or organized, it was difficult to navigate around with a screenreader.
