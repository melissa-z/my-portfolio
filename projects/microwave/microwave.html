Personas & Storyboarding
Microwave
How do we perceive and interact with microwaves?

The microwave oven is a common interface that provides a solution to heating up foods both quickly and efficiently. It attempts to provide a hands-off cooking experience that is effortless, mess-free, and quick, but that may not always be the reality of users' experiences.

To investigate how people perceive and interact with microwaves, I first began with a quick sketch of a microwave. A microwave's key components consist of a pull handle that opens a rectangular chamber, a number pad to set time with, a start button, a stop/clear button, and various preset setting options.

To better understand how people interact with the microwave, I observed three users using microwaves in their everyday lives. I then interviewed the users with a list of questions to gain insight about their experience, expectations, motives, and challenges while using the interface.

Questions
  1. On average, how many times a day do you use the microwave?
  2. What time(s) of day do you use the microwave?
  3. What kinds of foods do you use the microwave for?
  4. While the microwave is running, how do you spend your time?
  5. Which buttons on the microwave do you use most often?
  6. Are there buttons on the microwave that you’ve never used?
  7. How satisfied are you with the microwave's efficiency?
  8. How satisfied are you with the microwave's results? Do you find it necessary to add more time or take food out early?
  9. Do you clean your microwave? If yes, how often?
  10. How do you feel about the process of cooking in general?

Here are the recorded observations of each user's interactions with a microwave, as well as a summary of their interview responses.

Celine

Celine is a mother of two who cooks for the whole family.

  • She first transfers the leftover foods she wants to heat up from a tupperware container onto a plate.
  • She sets the time quickly and decisively using the number pad.
  • Just before pressing start, Celine hesitates, then leaves to get a napkin to cover the plate.
  • After carefully covering the plate, she presses the start button.
  • While microwave is running, Celine stretches her legs, then checks her phone for messages.
  • When the microwave first beeps and stops, Celine touches the plate and is unsatisfied with the results, and proceeds to add an extra 20 seeconds.
  • After the second round of heating, she touches the plate and is satisfied by the results.
  • Celine removes the now dirty napkin, and finally takes the plate to the table to begin to eat.

Celine usually uses the microwave 2 to 3 times a day, for breakfast and dinner. She finds the microwave quick to use and easy to clean up after. Celine says that while she enjoys cooking, she finds it very time consuming. If her kids are hungry or if she's exhausted, she resorts to microwaving leftovers.

However, Celine feels that it is difficult to estimate the heating tiime needed, and often needs to add more time multiple times to get the desired temperature.

There are many buttons on the microwave that Celine does not find useful. The only buttons that she uses on are "start" and the number pads to set time. She does not use any of the other buttons (eg. power level, preset cooking modes) – she is not sure what they do, and isn't interested in finding out. While the microwave runs, she stretches her legs and checks her phone for messages. She does not clean her microwave, unless something has exploded and made a mess.

Momoka

Momoka is a college student living at home.

  • Momoka gets a bowl of instant ramen from the pantry for lunch
  • She opens the lid covering partially, and then pours in cold water from the sink until bowl is half full
  • She paces bowl in microwave, then rotates the bowl a little bit to see the instructions, and quickly and firmly enters in the instructed time (4 minutes), and firmly presses “start”
  • While the ramen cooks, she does some squats in the kitchen, and then she leans against the counter and does some counter push-ups.
  • When the microwave timer is up, she very carefully lifts the steaming noodles out onto a table.
  • Momoka struggles to carry the bowl of noodles because of how hot it is.
  • She moves back to her desk to eat her lunch.
Momoka is a college student living at home. She says that she wants to learn to cook, but doesn’t do it often because it is time consuming. She uses the microwave 3 times a day, for breakfast, lunch, and snacks in the afternoon. In the morning with her breakfast, she microwaves her water for 20sec to lukewarmness. At lunch, she microwaves an instant ramen bowl, which takes 4 minutes. In the afternoon, she uses the microwave to make a snack, usually a mug cake.

Momoka finds the microwave to be a fast and easy cooking appliance, particularly for days where she has little time betweeen classes. She has a good idea of how long each item needs to be microwaved, and almost never finds it necessary to add more time/take food out early. The only buttons that she uses are the number pads, start, and power level. She doesn't know exactly what the other settings do. While the microwave is running, she does some counter push-ups and some squats in the kitchen. She does not clean her microwave.

Rafael

Rafael is a college student living off-campus on his own.

  • Rafael opens the freezer to grab a frozen pre-made burrito from the freezer and puts it directly into the microwave without a plate (the burrito is wrapped).
  • He quickly and decisively sets the time, changes the power level, and then presses the start button.
  • While microwave is running, leans on kitchen counter and checks his phone – reads emails, news, then plays game.
  • As soon as the microwave beeps, he takes out the burrito, but burns his hands a little and has to toss the burrito around like a hot potato.
  • He takes out a plate, unwraps the burrito, and puts it in the plate to eat.
Rafael is a college student living off campus. He describes cooking as “not my favorite”. On average, he uses the microwave 2-4 a day, for lunch, dinner, and snacks. At lunch he usually microwaves a frozen burrito between classes. For dinner, he sometimes uses the microwave to make frozen microwave meals or heat up leftovers. Later in the evenings, he often microwaves popcorn as a late night snack. The majority of the foods take over a minute to microwave, but he doesn’t usually know exactly how long to microwave them for, and he adjusts/adds more time as he goes.

His most used buttons are the power-level button, the “set 1min” shortcut, and start. He never uses any preset buttons for frozen meals, even when cooking frozen meals, and he also finds the light button unnecessary. While the microwave runs, he checks his phone for emails, news, and games. He does not clean his microwave unless something explodes and gets messy.
